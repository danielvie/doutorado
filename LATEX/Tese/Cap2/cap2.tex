Consider a switching affine system of the form

\noindent
\begin{equation}
    \dot{x}(t) = A_{\sigma(t)} x(t) + b_{\sigma(t)},
    \nonumber
\end{equation}


\noindent
the goal is to drive a system defined by an affine dynamic equation to an optimal 
periodic trajectory, so the first step is to generate this trajectory. This trajectory 
is submitted to restrictions imposed by the dynamic of the system and also switching time restrictions.


\section{Optimization}

Following the approach from \citeonline{patino2010predictive} we must establish a 
trajectory that yields the optimal time sequence \highlight{$\tau^\infty$}, the modes sequence \highlight{$\Omega^\infty$}, and its 
length \highlight{$s^\infty (1 < s^\infty < s_{max})$}. To reduce the oscillations around a desired operation point, we choose an integral 
quadratic criterion centered on the average reference value \highlight{$\bar{x}^\infty$}:

\begin{equation}
    \label{cap2_eq_optimization}
    J(\tau^{\infty} ,\Omega^{\infty} ,s^{\infty}, x_0) = \min_{\tau, \Omega, s} \int_0^{T_p} \Big[x(t) - \bar{x}^{\infty}\Big]^T Q \Big[x(t) - \bar{x}^{\infty}\Big] dt
\end{equation}

\noindent
where $\tau^\infty = \big[t_1, t_2, \dotsm, t_{s^\infty}\big]$, 
$\Omega^\infty = \big[\sigma_1, \sigma_2, \dotsm, \sigma_{s^\infty}\big]$, 
$Q=Q^T>0$ and $T_p$ is the period of the cycle.

Since the trajectory is cyclic we can impose the constraint:

\begin{equation}
    x(0) = x(T_p)
\end{equation}

\subsection{System Dynamics and Sequence}


    We are considering that the system dynamics will change after each switch command, this means that each 
    switching interval will have its dynamics represented by the matrices \highlight{$\big\{A_i, b_i\big\}$}. The same system 
    dynamic can be used again in different intervals. This is illustrated in figure \ref{cap2_fig_diferentes_dinamicas},
    where a sequence of dynamics is applied in the sequence of modes \highlight{$\big[4,2,1,2,3\big]$} to generate the cyclic trajectory.

\subsection{Constraints Definition}

    Once the list and sequence have been established, we need to apply the constraints,
    these constraints dictate the cycle's maximum duration
    
    \noindent
    \begin{equation}
        \label{cap2_tp_constraint}
        T_p \le T_{p,max}
    \end{equation}
    
    \noindent
    as well as the minimum time permitted between switches in modes in the form of

    \noindent
    \begin{equation}
        \label{cap2_time_constraint}
        (t_i - t_{i - 1}) \ge \Delta t_{i,min}\;, \quad i = 1,2,\dotso,N
    \end{equation}

    \noindent
    where \highlight{$T_p$} is the cycle period, \highlight{$T_{p,max}$} is the maximum cycle period,
    \highlight{$\Delta t_{i,min}$} is the minimum duration time permitted between switch \highlight{$i$}.

\subsection{Optimization with FMINCON}

    For optimization purposes, the function FMINCON is employed. This function works 
    in conjunction with a cost function. Its primary goal is to evaluate and 
    discern the most cost-effective result that respects the imposed constraints in \eqref{cap2_tp_constraint} and 
    \eqref{cap2_time_constraint}.
    
    The optimization algorithm solves the problem 
    
    \begin{equation}
        \min_x f(x) \;\text{such that} 
        \begin{cases}
            c(x) \le 0 \\ 
            ceq(x) = 0 \\
            A \cdot x \le B \\
            Aeq \cdot x = beq \\
            lb \le x \le ub
        \end{cases}
        \nonumber
    \end{equation}

    FMINCON is a method of optimization in MATLAB's libraries that implements the method 
    SQP (Sequential Quadratic Programming). 
    The basic SQP algorithm is described in Chapter 18 of \citeonline{nocedal2006numerical}.

    The cost function \highlight{$f(x)$} is designed to accept increments of switching times constrained by 
    \highlight{$lb \le x$} to evaluate the trajectory with the dwell-time constraints in place.
