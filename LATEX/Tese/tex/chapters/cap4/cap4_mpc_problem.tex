\section{MPC Problem}

Note that the equation \eqref{eq:cap4:linearization-final-error-result} relates to the equation \eqref{eq:cap3:dynamic-equation} as both describe the evolution of a
system over time. The equation \eqref{eq:cap3:dynamic-equation} represents the system's state and input at a given time step,
and the equation \eqref{eq:cap4:linearization-final-error-result} describes the error between the system's predicted and actual values at two different time points.

By referring to equation \eqref{eq:cap3:dynamic-equation}, several substitutions can be made to 
simplify the discussion. The variable \green{$x$ is replaced with $e$}, and \orange{$u$ with 
$\delta t$}. Additionally, the cycle index is denoted by \purple{$k$}. These substitutions result 
in equation \eqref{eq:cap4:linearization-final-error-result}.
\subsection{Cost Function}

The cost function for the MPC problem is given by

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:cap4:mpc-cost-function}
    J = \norma{e[N_p]}{P_f} \\
    + \sum^{N_p - 1}_{j=0}\norma{e[j]}{Q} + \norma{\delta t[j]}{R}
\end{equation}

\noindent
where \highlight{$Q = Q^T > 0$}, \highlight{$R = R^T > 0$}, \highlight{$e[j]$} is the 
error at cycle \highlight{$j$} and \highlight{$N_p$} is the prediction horizon spanning.


The error \highlight{$e[j]$} is computed by equation \eqref{eq:cap4:linearization-error-result-j} and the time vector
\highlight{$\delta t[j]$} is the difference between the switching times of the cycle and the nominal switching of the target trajectory  

\noindent
\begin{equation}
    \delta t[j] = \Big[t_{j,1} - \bar{t}_{j,1}, t_{j,2} - \bar{t}_{j,2}, ..., t_{j,N-1} - \bar{t}_{j,N-1}\Big]^T
\end{equation}

\subsection{Dwell-time Constraints}

We want to enforce a constraint on the dwell times where the switching times will the greater than a minimum value

\noindent
\begin{equation}
    (t_i - t_{i - 1}) \ge \Delta t_{i,min}\;, \quad i = 1,2,\dotso,N
\end{equation}

\noindent
we can write this as

\noindent
\begin{align}
    \delta t_1 &\ge - \Delta \bar{t}_1 + \Delta t_{1,min} \\
    \delta t_i - \delta t_{i-1} &\ge - \Delta \bar{t}_i + \Delta t_{i,min}\;, \quad i = 1,2,\dotso,N \\
    \delta t_{N-1} &\ge - \Delta \bar{t}_N + \Delta t_{N,min}
\end{align}

\noindent
with

\noindent
\begin{equation}
    \Delta \bar{t}_i = \bar{t}_i - \bar{t}_{i-1}
\end{equation}

Rearranging this to a matrix form we have

\begin{equation}
    L \delta t \ge c
\end{equation}

\noindent
with

\begin{equation}
    L =
    \left[
    \begin{matrix}
        1 & 0 & 0 & \dotsi & 0 & 0 \\
        -1 & 1 & 0 & \dotsi & 0 & 0 \\
        \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
        0 & 0 & 0 & \dotsi & -1 & 1 \\
        0 & 0 & 0 & \dotsi & 0 & -1 \\
    \end{matrix}
    \right]
\end{equation}


\begin{equation}
    \delta t =
    \left[
    \begin{matrix}
        \delta t_1 \\
        \delta t_2 \\
        \vdots \\
        \delta t_{N-1} \\
        \delta t_N \\
    \end{matrix}
    \right]
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
    c = \left[
    \begin{matrix}
        -\Delta \bar{t}_1 + \Delta t_{1,min} \\
        -\Delta \bar{t}_2 + \Delta t_{2,min} \\
        \vdots \\
        -\Delta \bar{t}_{N-1} + \Delta t_{N-1,min} \\
        -\Delta \bar{t}_{N} + \Delta t_{N,min} \\
    \end{matrix}
    \right]
\end{equation}


This constraint is related to the \highlight{$j$th} cycle of the system, so the notation of $\delta t$ can be rewritten as

\begin{equation}
    \delta t[j] = 
    \left[
        \begin{matrix}
            \delta t_{j,1} \\
            \delta t_{j,2} \\
            \vdots \\
            \delta t_{j,N-1} \\
            \delta t_{j,N}
        \end{matrix}
    \right]
\end{equation}

\noindent
To use this with the constraints \highlight{$S \mathbf{\hat{u}}[k] \le b$}, this can be rearranged as

\begin{equation*}
    -L \delta t[j] \le -c
\end{equation*}
