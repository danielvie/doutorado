Consider a system with discrete-time dynamics described by

    \begin{equation}
        x[k + 1] = A x[k] + B u[k]
        \label{eq:cap3:dynamic-equation}
    \end{equation}

\noindent
where \highlight{$x[k] \in \mathbb{R}^n$} and \highlight{$u[k] \in \mathbb{R}^p$} are 
the state and control vectors at the $k$th time instant.

The goal is to drive the state \highlight{$x[k]$} to the origin from a given initial condition while respecting
state and control constraints in the form

\begin{equation}
    \label{eq:cap3:state-constraint-set}
    x[k] \in \mathcal{X}
\end{equation}
    
\begin{equation}
    \label{eq:cap3:input-constraint-set}
    u[k] \in \mathcal{U}
\end{equation}

\noindent
where $\mathcal{U}$ and $\mathcal{X}$ are polyhedral sets containing the origin in their interior.
It is assumed that $\mathcal{U}$ and $\mathcal{X}$ are described in half-space representation as


\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{U} = \left\{
        u \in \mathbb{R}^p : S_u u \leq b_u	
    \right\}
\end{equation}

\noindent
and

\begin{equation}
    \label{eq:cap3:polyhedral-state-constraint}
    \mathcal{X} = \left\{
        x \in \mathbb{R}^n : S_x x \leq b_x
    \right\}
\end{equation}

\noindent
with \highlight{$S_u$, $b_u$} and \highlight{$S_x$, $b_x$} of suitable dimensions.

\section{Cost Function}


At each time $k$, the control action $u[k]$ will be determined by solving an 
optimization problem with the cost given by


\begin{equation}
    J = \blue{\norma{\hat{x}[k + N | k]}{P_f}} + \orange{\sum^{N-1}_{i=0} \Bigg[ \norma{\hat{x}[k + i | k]}{Q} + \norma{\hat{u}[k + i | k]}{R} \Bigg]}
\end{equation}

\noindent
where $\hat{x}[k+i|k]$ are predicted values of the state and control vectors, which are 
related through the following equations:

\noindent
\begin{equation}
    \hat{x}[k|k] = x[k]
\end{equation}

\noindent
\begin{equation}
    \hat{x}[k + i + 1|k] = A \hat{x}[k+i|k] + Bu
\end{equation}

\noindent
\begin{equation}
    \hat{x}[k + i|k], \quad i =  0, 1, \dotsm, N-1
\end{equation}


The expression for \highlight{$J$} consists of a \blue{terminal cost} term and a trajectory 
\orange{cost term}. The next step is to express the predicted states as an explicit function of the current state and of the sequence of predicted inputs. This prediction model will then be used to rewrite both the constraints and the cost in terms of the optimization variable.

The weighting matrices \highlight{$P_f$}, \highlight{$Q$}, and \highlight{$R$} are symmetric and 
positive-definite. Usually, \highlight{$P_f$} is chosen such that the terminal cost 
\highlight{$\norma{x}{P_f}$} corresponds to the value function of an infinite-horizon 
Discrete-Time Linear Quadratic Regulator (DLQR) designed using the weighting matrices $Q$ and $R$.

By using the notation

\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{\hat{u}}[k] = 
    \left[
        \begin{matrix}
            \hat{u}[k | k] \\		
            \hat{u}[k + 1 | k] \\		
            \vdots \\
            \hat{u}[k + N-1 | k]
        \end{matrix}
    \right]
    \in \mathbb{R}^{pN},
    \mathbf{\hat{x}}[k] = 
    \left[
        \begin{matrix}
            \hat{x}[k + 1| k] \\ 
            \hat{x}[k + 2| k] \\ 
            \vdots \\
            \hat{x}[k + N | k]
        \end{matrix}
    \right]
    \in \mathbb{R}^{nN}
\end{equation}

\noindent
the cost function can be written in the form

\noindent
\begin{equation}
    J(\mathbf{\hat{x}}[k], \mathbf{\hat{u}}[k]) = \norma{x[k]}{Q} + 
    \norma{\mathbf{\hat{x}}[k]}{\mathbf{Q}} + \norma{\mathbf{\hat{u}}[k]}{\mathbf{R}}
\end{equation}

\noindent
where

\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{Q} = 
    \left[
        \begin{matrix}
            Q & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
            0 & Q & \cdots & 0 \\
            \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
            0 & 0 & \cdots & P_f
        \end{matrix}
    \right]
    = 
    \left[
        \begin{matrix}
            diag_{N-1}(Q) & 0 \\
            0 & P_f
        \end{matrix}
    \right]
    \in \mathbb{R}^{nN \times nN}
\end{equation}

\noindent
and

\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{R} = 
    \left[
        \begin{matrix}
        R & 0 & \dotsm & 0 \\
        0 & R & \dotsm & 0 \\
        \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
        0 & 0 & \dotsm & R
        \end{matrix}
    \right] \in \mathbb{R}^{pN \times pN}
\end{equation}
