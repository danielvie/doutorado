\section{Prediction Equation}

The cost function introduced above depends on the predicted state sequence. To obtain an optimization problem in terms of the input sequence, this section writes the predicted states explicitly as a function of the current state and of the predicted inputs.

Consider the stacked vectors

\begin{equation}
	\mathbf{\hat{u}}[k] = \left[
		\begin{matrix}
			\hat{u}[k | k] \\
			\hat{u}[k + 1 | k] \\
			\vdots \\
			\hat{u}[k + N-1 | k]
		\end{matrix}
	\right],
	\qquad
	\mathbf{\hat{x}}[k] =
	\left[
		\begin{matrix}
			\hat{x}[k + 1 | k] \\
			\hat{x}[k + 2 | k] \\
			\vdots \\
			\hat{x}[k + N | k]
		\end{matrix}
	\right].
\end{equation}

\noindent
The predicted state evolves according to

\noindent
\begin{equation}
	\label{eq:cap3:one-step-prediction}
	\hat{x}[k + i + 1 | k] = A\hat{x}[k+i | k] + B \hat{u}[k+i | k],
	\quad i = 0, 1, \ldots, N-1,
\end{equation}

\noindent
with initial prediction condition

\noindent
\begin{equation}
	\hat{x}[k|k] = x[k].
\end{equation}

By repeated substitution of \eqref{eq:cap3:one-step-prediction}, the predicted state at step \highlight{$i$} can be written as

\begin{equation}
	\hat{x}[k+i|k] = A^i x[k] + \sum_{j=0}^{i-1} A^{i - 1 -j} B \hat{u}[k+j|k],
	\quad i = 1,2,\ldots,N.
\end{equation}

\noindent
Therefore,

\noindent
\begin{align}
	\hat{x}[k+1|k] &= A x[k] + B \hat{u}[k|k] \\
	\hat{x}[k+2|k] &= A^2 x[k] + A B \hat{u}[k|k] + B \hat{u}[k+1|k] \\
	&\vdots \\
	\hat{x}[k+N|k] &= A^N x[k] + A^{N - 1} B \hat{u}[k|k] + \cdots + A B \hat{u}[k +N - 2|k] + B \hat{u}[k + N - 1|k].
\end{align}

\noindent
Stacking these equations gives

\noindent
\begin{equation}
	\left[
		\begin{matrix}
			\hat{x}[k+1|k] \\
			\hat{x}[k+2|k] \\
			\vdots \\
			\hat{x}[k+N|k]
		\end{matrix}
	\right] =
	\left[
		\begin{matrix}
			A \\
			A^2 \\
			\vdots \\
			A^N
		\end{matrix}
	\right]
	x[k] +
	\left[
		\begin{matrix}
			B & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
			AB & B & \cdots & 0 \\
			\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
			A^{N-1}B & A^{N-2}B & \cdots & B
		\end{matrix}
	\right]
	\left[
		\begin{matrix}
			\hat{u}[k|k] \\
			\hat{u}[k+1|k]\\
			\vdots \\
			\hat{u}[k+N-1|k]
		\end{matrix}
	\right].
\end{equation}

\noindent
This can be written in compact form as

\noindent
\begin{equation}
	\label{eq:cap3:stacked-state-prediction}
	\mathbf{\hat{x}}[k] = \mathbf{A}x[k] + \mathbf{B}\mathbf{\hat{u}}[k],
\end{equation}

\noindent
where

\noindent
\begin{equation}
	\mathbf{A} = \left[
		\begin{matrix}
			A \\
			A^2 \\
			\vdots \\
			A^N
		\end{matrix}
	\right],
	\qquad
	\mathbf{B} = \left[
		\begin{matrix}
			B  & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
			AB & B & \cdots & 0 \\
			\vdots  & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
			A^{N-1}B & A^{N-2}B & \cdots & B
		\end{matrix}
	\right].
\end{equation}

Equation \eqref{eq:cap3:stacked-state-prediction} is the condensed prediction model used in the following sections to lift the constraints and rewrite the optimization problem in terms of the input sequence.
