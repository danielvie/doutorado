\section{Statement of the problem}

Considering a plant of an affine switched system described by: 

\noindent
\begin{equation}
    \label{eq_continuous_time_dynamics}
    \dot{x}(t) = A_{\sigma(t)} x(t) + b_{\sigma(t)}
\end{equation}

\begin{figure}[h]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[]{Cap4/fig/desenho_cap4_3.eps}
    \caption{Switching signals}\label{cap2_linearizacao}
\end{figure}

We assume the existence of a periodic switching signal \highlight{$\bar{\sigma}(t)$}
associated with a periodic trajectory \highlight{$\bar{x}(t)$}, as obtained in 
Chapter \ref{cap2-determination-of-target-periodic-traj}. Our objective is to introduce perturbations 
in the switching instants such that the trajectory \highlight{$x(t)$} converges to the reference trajectory \highlight{$\bar{x}(t)$}.

Figure \ref{cap2_linearizacao} displays the \highlight{$j_{th}$} cycle of the switching signals \highlight{$\bar{\sigma}(t)$} and \highlight{$\sigma(t)$},
which comprise \highlight{$N$} switching actions. Within this cycle, the \highlight{$i_{th}$} switching times of \highlight{$\bar{\sigma}(t)$} and 
\highlight{$\sigma(t)$} are denoted by \highlight{$\bar{t}_{j, i}$} and \highlight{$t_{j, i}$}, respectively.
The relation between \highlight{$\bar{t}_{j, i}$} and \highlight{$t_{j, i}$} is given by

\noindent
\begin{equation}
    t_{j, i} = \bar{t}_{j, i} + \delta t_{j, i}
\end{equation}

To do this using predictive control, we need to find a linear equation that relates the switching times and the resulting system at the end of one cycle.
