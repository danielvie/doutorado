We assume the existence of a signal sequence $\sigma(t)$ with switching instants $\bar{t}_{j, i}$, 
where this periodic switching signal $\sigma(t)$ is associated with a periodic trajectory $\bar{x}(t)$, as obtained in 
Chapter \ref{cap2-determination-of-target-periodic-traj}. Our objective is to introduce perturbations such that the trajectory 
converges to the reference trajectory.

In FIGURE \ref{cap2_linearizacao}, we represent a periodic signal where $\bar{t}_{j, i}$ are
the switching times of the reference trajectory and $t_{j, i}$ are the switching times of the current trajectory of
the system. The relation between $\bar{t}_{j, i}$ and $t_{j, i}$ is given by

\noindent
\begin{equation}
    t_{j, i} = \bar{t}_{j, i} + \delta t_{j, i}
\end{equation}

The trajectory is represented by 
FIGURE \ref{cap2_linearizacao} displays the j-th cycle of the trajectory that contains $N$ switching actions.


Considering a plant of an affine switched system described by: 

\begin{equation}
    \label{eq_continuous_time_dynamics}
    \dot{x}(t) = A_{\sigma(t)} x(t) + b_{\sigma(t)}
\end{equation}

The goal is to drive the system from $x(t)$ to $\bar{x}(t)$ by injecting perturbations on the switching times $t_{j, i}$.
To do this using predictive control, we need to find a linear equation that relates the switching times and the resulting system at the end of one cycle.
