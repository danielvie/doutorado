\subsection{Double Integrator}


\subsubsection{Parameters of the Simulation}

This example describes the double integrator model used in \citeonline{matheus2018} of the form

\begin{equation}
    \dot{x}(t) = A_x x(t) + B_c u(t)
\end{equation}

\noindent
where

\begin{equation}
    \label{cap4_eq_dynamics_integrador}
    A_c = \left[
        \begin{matrix}
            0 & 1 \\
            0 & 0 \\
        \end{matrix}
    \right],
    B_c = \left[
        \begin{matrix}
            0 \\
            1
        \end{matrix}
    \right]
\end{equation}

We are using the associated input signal computed by \citeonline{matheus2020_artigo} and 
the resulting trajectory is defined by

\begin{equation}
    \label{cap4_eq_signal_integrador}
    u_r = \left[
        \begin{matrix}
            1 \\
            0 \\
            -1 \\
            0
        \end{matrix}
    \right],
    T_s = \left[
        \begin{matrix}
            0 &
            2 &
            3 &
            5 &
            6
        \end{matrix}
    \right]
\end{equation}

\noindent
where \highlight{$u_r$} is the control command and \highlight{$T_s$} is the switching time values for the cyclic trajectory

The original problem has the same dynamics during all operations, so we need to construct the associated \highlight{$A_i, b_i$} for each operation mode. 
One solution is to create 3 unique pairs

\begin{equation}
    \label{cap4-eq-dynamic-system-integrador}
    \begin{matrix}
        \green{A_1 = \left[ \begin{matrix} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \\ \end{matrix} \right]} &
        \orange{A_2 = \left[ \begin{matrix} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \\ \end{matrix} \right]} &
        \blue{A_3 = \left[ \begin{matrix} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \\ \end{matrix} \right]} &
        \\
        \\
        \green{b_1 = \left[ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right]} &
        \orange{b_2 = \left[ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right]} &
        \blue{b_3 = \left[ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ -1 \end{matrix} \right]} &
    \end{matrix}
    \nonumber
\end{equation}


\noindent
and the index for the operation mode \highlight{$\Omega = \big[1, 2, 3, 2\big]$}, with 
reference state vector for this trajectory is \highlight{$\bar{x}_{ref} = \big[2, -1\big]$}, 
the maximum value for the period of the trajectory cycle \highlight{$T_{p_{max}} = 1$}
and initial state of the nominal target trajectory \highlight{$x_{0} = \big[1.5, -1\big]$}.

\subsubsection{Prediction Matrices}

From \eqref{eq_Phi} and \eqref{eq_Gamma} we construct

\begin{equation}
    \Phi =
    \left[
    \begin{matrix}
        1 & 6 \\
        0 & 1
    \end{matrix}
    \right]
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
    \Gamma =
    \left[
    \begin{matrix}
        4 & 3 & -1 \\
        1 & 1 & -1
    \end{matrix}
    \right]
\end{equation}

\noindent
with the switching time constraints

\begin{equation}
    c = 
    \left[
        \begin{matrix}
            -\Delta t_{r_1} + \Delta t_{r_{1, min}} \\
            -\Delta t_{r_2} + \Delta t_{r_{2, min}} \\
            \vdots \\
            -\Delta t_{r_{N-1}} + \Delta t_{r_{{N-1}, min}} \\
            -\Delta t_{r_N} + \Delta t_{r_{N, min}} \\
        \end{matrix}
    \right] = 
    \left[
        \begin{matrix}
            - 2 + 1.625 \\
            - 1 + 0.635 \\
            - 2 + 1.625 \\
            - 1 + 0.625 \\
        \end{matrix}
    \right] =
    \left[
        \begin{matrix}
            - 0.375 \\
            - 0.365 \\
            - 0.375 \\
            - 0.365 \\
        \end{matrix}
    \right]
\end{equation}

($N_p = 1$, $N_p = 2$ and $N_p = 3$) as defined in Section \ref{section_reasibility_region}. 
A larger prediction horizon reflected in a larger feasibility region enables the use of the predictive control law 
with initial conditions farther from the target.
