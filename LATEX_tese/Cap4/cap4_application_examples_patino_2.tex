\subsubsection{init - configuracoes da simulacao}

The values used to simulate the Buck-boost converter example are:

- $\Omega$: $\Big[1, 2, 4, 8, 3, 1, 5, 8, 5\Big]$

- $s_{max}$ is set to 12.

- The dynamics of the system are defined by

\begin{equation}
    \begin{matrix}
        A_i = \left[ 
            \begin{matrix} 
                0 &       0 & \Big(u_2(i)-u_1(i)\Big)/C1 \\ 
                0 & 0 & \Big(u_3(i)-u_2(i)\Big)/C2 \\ 
                \Big(u_1(i)-u_2(i)\Big)/L & \Big(u_2(i)-u_3(i)\Big)/L & -R/L \\ 
            \end{matrix} \right] \\
            \\
        b_i = \left[ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ \Big(E/L\Big) u_3(i) \end{matrix} \right]
    \end{matrix}
\end{equation}

\noindent
where R = 10.0 $\Omega$, L = 10.0 $mH$, $C_1$ = 40.0 $\mu F$, $C_2$ = 40.0*1e-6 $\mu F$, E  = 30.0 $V$
