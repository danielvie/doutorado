Consider a switching affine system of the form

\noindent
\begin{equation}
    \dot{x}(t) = A_{\sigma(t)} x(t) + b_{\sigma(t)},
    \nonumber
\end{equation}

\noindent
the goal is to drive a system defined by an affine dynamic equation to an optimal 
periodic trajectory, so the first step is to generate this trajectory. This trajectory 
is submitted to restrictions imposed by the dynamic of the system and also switching time restrictions.


\section{Optimization}

Following the approach from \citeonline{patino2010predictive} we must establish a 
trajectory that yields the optimal time sequence \highlight{$\tau^\infty$}, the modes sequence \highlight{$\Omega^\infty$}, and its 
length \highlight{$s^\infty (1 < s^\infty < s_{max})$}. To reduce the oscillations around a desired operation point, we choose an integral 
quadratic criterion centered on the average reference value \highlight{$\bar{x}^\infty$}:

\begin{equation}
    \label{cap2_eq_optimization}
    J(\tau^{\infty} ,\Omega^{\infty} ,s^{\infty}, x_0) = \min_{\tau, \Omega, s} \int_0^{T_p} \Big[x(t) - \bar{x}^{\infty}\Big]^T Q \Big[x(t) - \bar{x}^{\infty}\Big] dt
\end{equation}

\noindent
where $\tau^\infty = \big[t_1, t_2, \dotsm, t_{s^\infty}\big]$, 
$\Omega^\infty = \big[\sigma_1, \sigma_2, \dotsm, \sigma_{s^\infty}\big]$, 
$Q=Q^T>0$ and $T_p$ is the period of the cycle.

Since the trajectory is cyclic we can impose the constraint:

\begin{equation}
    x(0) = x(T_p)
\end{equation}

\subsection{System Dynamics and Sequence}

    To begin, we create a list where each of its elements signifies the dynamics of 
    the system. This is exemplified by the matrices A, B, C, and D. The order in which 
    the elements appear in this list is defined by the sequence of modes. In simpler 
    terms, the positioning of these elements signifies the order they will be executed.

    In Figure \refeq{cap2_fig_diferentes_dinamicas}, we see a trajectory example. 
    For this illustration, the system's list of dynamics is 
    $\Big\{\big[A_1,b_1\big], \big[A_2,b_2\big], \big[A_3,b_3\big], \big[A_4,b_4\big]\Big\}$,
    and the sequence of modes is $\big[4,2,1,2,3\big]$. It's worth noting that a particular system 
    dynamic can be employed multiple times or might not be used at all, depending on 
    the desired target trajectory. 
    The sequence of modes and the maximum period for the cycle are predetermined for this problem and
    the goal is to achieve the best trajectory that minimizes the cost function.

\subsection{Optimization with FMINCON}

    For optimization purposes, the function FMINCON is employed. This function works 
    in conjunction with a cost function. The cost function is designed to accept switching 
    times generated from the optimization process. Its primary goal is to evaluate and 
    discern the most cost-effective result that respects the imposed constraints.

    FMINCON is a method of optimization in MATLAB's libraries that implements the method 
    SQP (Sequential Quadratic Programming). 
    The basic SQP algorithm is described in Chapter 18 of \citeonline{nocedal2006numerical}.

\subsection{Cost Function and Trajectory Simulation}

    Taking a closer look at the cost function, its role is to simulate the trajectory using 
    the dynamics taken from the previously defined list, all in line with the mode sequence. 
    This trajectory is cyclic and to enforce this cyclic nature, the initial state's condition 
    is calculated in such a way that the end product is a cyclic trajectory.

    The method of determining the cost is by computing the integral of the trajectory, 
    centered around the average reference value, denoted as \highlight{$\bar{x}^\infty$}. The result 
    is compliant with the system's dynamic constraints and also matches the properties 
    of the final trajectory.
